How OMNIX QUANTUM Works

OMNIX sits between your signal generation (quant models, analysts, third-party providers) and trade execution. Every proposed trade must pass through our 4-layer validation pipeline.

Layer 1: Regime Detection

Purpose: Identify if the market is in a fundamentally different state than when your strategy was designed.

How it works: Analyzes volatility patterns, correlation structures, and liquidity conditions. If current regime doesn't match historical training data, the trade is flagged.

Result: Prevents strategies from trading in conditions they weren't designed for.

Layer 2: Coherence & Edge Validation

Purpose: Verify that the proposed trade makes sense given current market microstructure and your strategy's historical edge.

How it works: Checks if the signal aligns with your strategy's known patterns, if market liquidity supports the trade size, and if execution costs won't erode expected returns.

Result: Blocks trades that look profitable on paper but will fail in real execution.

Layer 3: Monte Carlo Risk Simulation

Purpose: Simulate thousands of potential outcomes to understand tail risk and maximum drawdown scenarios.

How it works: Runs 10,000+ simulations of the proposed trade under different market scenarios, calculating Value-at-Risk (VaR) and Conditional VaR (CVaR). If worst-case scenarios exceed risk limits, trade is rejected.

Result: Protects against low-probability, high-impact losses that can destroy portfolios.

Layer 4: Consensus Gate

Purpose: Final decision point that requires all previous layers to approve before execution.

How it works: Aggregates signals from Layers 1-3. Only if all layers give green light does the trade proceed. Any single red flag blocks execution and logs the reason.

Result: Creates an immutable audit trail showing exactly why each trade was approved or rejected.
